Site reliability engineering (SRE)

Site Reliability Engineering in eCommerce: adidas, Always On Like an ER

In the fast-paced world of eCommerce, downtime is not just a technical hiccup—it’s lost revenue, broken customer trust, and a blow to brand reputation. At adidas, a brand that champions performance and precision, the responsibility to keep digital systems healthy and always available falls to a team often operating behind the scenes: Site Reliability Engineers (SREs).

If you’ve ever wondered who ensures your sneakers reach the cart without glitches or why that limited-edition drop didn’t crash the site, you’re thinking about SREs.

What is Site Reliability Engineering?

Site Reliability Engineering is the discipline that bridges the gap between software engineering and IT operations. Think of it as the emergency room of tech infrastructure. SREs work tirelessly to ensure that systems are reliable, scalable, and resilient, even under pressure.

While traditional roles focus on building features or managing systems, SREs specialize in making sure everything keeps running—especially when things go wrong.

SRE at adidas: More Than Uptime

In the context of adidas eCommerce, SREs are at the heart of digital excellence. They work across global platforms, managing traffic spikes during product launches, Black Friday sales, and flash campaigns—all without dropping the ball.

Preventing Failures: The Preventive Care

Just as a hospital doesn’t wait for patients to collapse before acting, SREs don’t wait for systems to fail. They embed failure prevention into their everyday processes:

  • Chaos Engineering: Introducing controlled failures to test system resilience.
  • Load Testing: Ensuring infrastructure can handle high-demand scenarios, like Yeezy drops.
  • Capacity Planning: Predicting future resource needs before the system feels strain.

This proactive mindset ensures that adidas platforms can handle unpredictable conditions—just like an ER preps for a mass casualty event, even if it never comes.

Resilience: Digital Immunity

Resilience means more than uptime; it’s about bouncing back when something inevitably goes wrong. In eCommerce, failures might look like slow checkout pages, broken search functions, or delayed inventory syncs.

SREs design systems with graceful degradation: even when one part of the system fails, others continue to work. It’s like a trauma surgeon stabilizing a patient while waiting for a specialist—keeping the essentials functioning until full recovery.

Key tools here include:

  • Redundancy (e.g., multiple servers or data centers)
  • Failovers (automatic switching to backup systems)
  • Circuit Breakers (preventing cascading failures)

Observability: The Diagnostic Tools

Doctors don’t guess—they use tools like MRIs and blood tests. SREs are no different. Observability is their diagnostic toolkit, built with:

  • Metrics: CPU usage, latency, throughput
  • Logs: System behavior snapshots in real-time
  • Tracing: End-to-end journey of a request through services

At adidas, this means being able to trace a customer’s experience from homepage to checkout, identifying friction points before users even notice them.

Observability empowers the team to ask, “Why did this fail?” and more importantly, “How can we prevent it from happening again?”

The Challenges of Being an SRE at adidas

Like ER teams, SREs must remain calm under pressure. The role is both thrilling and demanding:

The Tough Parts:

  • Always-On Culture: The platform is global, and issues don’t wait for business hours.
  • Balancing Innovation and Stability: New features can bring instability; SREs often walk a tightrope between agility and caution.
  • Incident Response Fatigue: High-severity incidents can be draining, both mentally and emotionally.

The Rewards:

  • Making Real Impact: One fix can affect millions of customers instantly.
  • Building Resilience: Every failure is a learning opportunity.
  • Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration: SREs work with developers, product managers, security teams—everyone.

Conclusion: Tech’s Emergency Room, Always Ready

At adidas eCommerce, the digital experience is a vital part of the brand journey. Just like a hospital ER saves lives, SREs save customer experiences, ensure business continuity, and protect revenue.

They don’t just keep systems alive—they make them stronger with every challenge. With observability as their X-ray, resilience as their immune system, and automation as their scalpel, SREs at adidas are the unsung heroes behind every frictionless drop and flawless checkout.

So the next time you score that limited-edition sneaker without a hiccup, thank an SRE.
