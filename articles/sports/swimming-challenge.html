The Training Plan: Structure Meets Discipline

You don't wake up one day and swim 33 kilometers. You build toward it systematically, respecting the process and trusting the plan.

Phase 1: Foundation Building (Months 1-3)

  • Goal: Build base endurance and perfect technique
  • Volume: 3-4 swims per week, 2-4km per session
  • Focus: Breathing efficiency, stroke mechanics, body position
  • Mindset: Patience—technique before speed or distance

Phase 2: Volume Accumulation (Months 4-6)

  • Goal: Gradually increase weekly swimming volume
  • Volume: 4-5 swims per week, building to 5-8km per session
  • Focus: Endurance, pacing strategy, mental toughness
  • Key milestone: Complete first 10km continuous swim

Phase 3: Ultra-Distance Preparation (Months 7-9)

  • Goal: Adapt to ultra-distance demands
  • Volume: 5-6 swims per week, long sessions 10-20km
  • Focus: Nutrition during swim, mental strategies, open water acclimatization
  • Key milestone: Complete 20km+ training swim

Phase 4: Taper & Peak (Months 10-12)

  • Goal: Arrive fresh and confident on race day
  • Volume: Reduce volume, maintain intensity
  • Focus: Recovery, visualization, race strategy refinement
  • Mindset: Trust the training—the work is done

Building Resilience: The Mental Game

Physical training is only half the battle. An ultra swim of this magnitude is as much a mental challenge as a physical one. The water becomes a mirror—it reflects back everything: your doubts, your fears, your strength, your determination.

Mental Strategies for Ultra Swimming:

  • Chunking: Don't think about 33km. Think about the next 500m. Then the next.
  • Mantras: Simple phrases repeated with each stroke: "Strong and smooth," "I belong here," "One more stroke"
  • Visualization: See yourself finishing before you start. Feel the accomplishment.
  • Pain management: Acknowledge discomfort without letting it control you
  • Present moment focus: Not the kilometers behind, not the distance ahead—just this stroke, right now

Training teaches you that resilience isn't about never struggling—it's about continuing despite the struggle. Some days the water feels like home. Other days it feels like you're swimming through concrete. You swim anyway.

Discipline: The Bridge Between Goals and Accomplishment

Motivation gets you into the pool on day one. Discipline gets you into the pool on day 200 when it's cold, you're tired, and a hundred excuses are whispering in your ear.

Discipline is:

  • Setting the alarm: And getting up when it rings, no snooze button
  • Preparing the night before: Bag packed, gear ready, no morning friction
  • Showing up tired: The session still counts, even if it's not perfect
  • Saying no to distractions: Protecting training time like it's sacred
  • Celebrating small wins: Acknowledging every session completed
  • Learning from setbacks: Missed sessions happen—get back on track immediately

Discipline isn't punishment—it's self-respect. It's honoring the commitment you made to yourself.
